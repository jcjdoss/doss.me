Webex Calling Migration: Week 3

Migrating 400+ users off legacy telephony while keeping the lights on is the IT equivalent of changing a tire at 60mph. We're at 72% and only two "but my fax machine" tickets this week. I'll take it.

Feb 1, 2026 Life

ADHD and the Open Tab Lifestyle

Current browser tab count: 47. Each one represents a thought I was absolutely going to get back to. The lie we tell ourselves isn't that we'll close them. It's that we need them open.

I've tried the "tab management" apps. I've tried the "only 5 tabs" rule. I've tried bookmark folders. None of it works because the tabs aren't the problem. The tabs are how my brain maps the world. Closing them feels like closing possibilities. So 47 it is.

Why Government IT Doesn't Have to Suck

Hot take that shouldn't be hot: government employees deserve the same quality tech experience as private sector workers. ServiceNow intake optimization isn't sexy. But 1,000 county employees not wanting to throw their laptops out the window? That's the dream.

We just redesigned our ticket intake process. Reduced average resolution time. The reaction from staff wasn't "wow great IT initiative." It was just... fewer complaints. And that's exactly how you know it's working. The best IT is the kind nobody notices.

Jan 15, 2026 Life

Saying GREAT Instead of Fine

Someone asked how I was doing today. I said "GREAT." They did a visible double-take. That's the whole point.

"Fine" is the verbal equivalent of a shrug. It communicates nothing and expects nothing back. "GREAT" is a choice. It's slightly aggressive optimism. It forces a reaction. People either smile or look confused, and both of those are better than the nothing you get from "fine."

Life is too short for lukewarm responses.
